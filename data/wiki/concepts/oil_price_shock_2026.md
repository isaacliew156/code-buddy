# Oil Price Shock 2026

The 2026 oil price shock was triggered by Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz following the US-Iran war outbreak, representing the worst energy crisis since the 1970s oil shocks.

## Price Levels (April 2026)
- **WTI Crude**: ~$112/barrel
- **Brent Crude**: ~$110/barrel

## Timeline and Key Events

### Pre-War Levels
- **Late February 2026**: Oil trading in $70-80/barrel range

### Shock Events
- **March 4, 2026**: Iran effectively closes Strait of Hormuz, removing ~20% of global daily oil and LNG supply
- **Mid-March**: Brent briefly retests $120/barrel
- **March 27**: Crude surges above $110/barrel following broad sell-off
- **April 2**: WTI jumps 12% in single session to $112.10 after President Trump's televised address vowing to continue operations for 2-3 more weeks
- **Early April**: Prices stabilize around $109-112 range with volatility tied to ceasefire speculation

## Market Impact
The oil shock has been the single most important macro variable in 2026, with dramatic consequences:
- **Energy Sector**: +18% in March 2026 alone, top-performing sector
- **Inflation Pressure**: Fed revised inflation projections upward due to oil prices
- **Consumer Discretionary**: -12% in March, worst performer due to fuel costs and recession fears

## Strategic Context
- **Strait of Hormuz**: Vital chokepoint through which 20% of global oil and LNG supply flows
- **Historical Comparison**: IEA warned this could be worst energy crisis since 1970s oil shocks
- **Geopolitical Factors**: OPEC dynamics, Saudi Arabia's response, and diplomatic negotiations all influence market
