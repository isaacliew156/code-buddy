# Iranian Military Response (2026)

## Overview
Iran's military response to Operation Epic Fury involved a comprehensive campaign of missile and drone strikes targeting US, Israeli, and allied forces across the Middle East.

## Primary Attack Vectors

### Ballistic Missiles
- Used against Israeli cities and military targets
- Targets include IDF's Kirya headquarters in Tel Aviv (April 4, 2026)
- Range: Capable of reaching Israel and Gulf states
- Accuracy: Demonstrated ability to hit specific military installations

### Drone Strikes
- Used against US bases in region
- Example: Ali Al Salem Air Base in Kuwait (April 6, 2026) injured 15 US personnel
- Swarm tactics: Multiple drones deployed simultaneously
- Low-cost asymmetric warfare

## Geographic Targets

### Israel
- **Ramat Gan**: Missile barrage killed two Israeli civilians (March 17, 2026)
- **Tel Aviv**: IDF headquarters targeted
- **Military sites**: Various Israeli defense installations

### US Military Bases
- **Qatar**: US base attacked (June 2025, pre-war)
- **Kuwait**: Ali Al Salem Air Base (April 2026)
- **UAE, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, Iraq, Oman, Jordan**: US embassies and installations targeted

### Gulf States (Non-Belligerents)
- **UAE**: Direct attacks despite neutrality
- **Qatar**: 17% of LNG capacity destroyed (5-year impact)
- **Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman**: Multiple strikes

### UK Targets
- **Bahrain, Qatar, Cyprus**: UK bases attacked
- **Strategic messaging**: Targeting European presence

### NATO Airspace Violation
- **Turkey**: Missile entered Turkish airspace, intercepted by NATO defenses (landed in Hatay Province)
- **Significance**: Forced NATO defensive action despite European non-involvement

## Economic Warfare

### Energy Targets
- **Qatar LNG**: 17% capacity destroyed, major economic impact
- **Regional energy**: Attacks on Gulf state infrastructure
- **Psychological impact**: Demonstrating ability to damage global energy supply

### Strait of Hormuz Closure
- **March 2026**: Iran closes strategic waterway
- **Economic leverage**: 20% of world's oil transits through strait
- **Global impact**: Oil prices surge above $100 per barrel

## Asymmetric Warfare

### Proxy Forces
- **Hezbollah**: Significantly weakened but engaged in parallel conflict
- **Houthis**: Yemen-based militants attacking Israel, threatening Bab al-Mandeb Strait
- **Shiite militias**: Zainebiyoun (Pakistan), Fatemiyoun (Afghanistan)

### Missile Technology
- **Underground facilities**: Stored highly enriched uranium in facilities that survived previous strikes
- **IAEA assessment**: Could not confirm peaceful nuclear activities (February 2026)
- **US intelligence**: Claimed missiles capable of reaching US

## Strategic Objectives

### Deterrence
- Demonstrating capability to strike back despite heavy losses
- Imposing costs on US allies in region
- Maintaining strategic leverage through Strait of Hormuz control

### Political Messaging
- Targeting Gulf states to undermine regional support for US
- Forcing NATO defensive action through Turkish airspace violation
- Demonstrating resilience despite leadership decapitation

### Economic Pressure
- Using energy weaponry to influence global markets
- Creating economic pain for supporting nations
- Leveraging oil prices for diplomatic advantage

## Effectiveness Assessment

### Successes
- **Economic disruption**: Significant impact on global oil markets
- **Regional deterrence**: Demonstrated reach across Middle East
- **Political messaging**: Forced NATO defensive action

### Limitations
- **Leadership damage**: Supreme Leader killed, leadership fractured
- **Infrastructure destruction**: Major petrochemical complexes damaged
- **International isolation**: Limited support from China and Russia
- **No strategic breakthrough**: Unable to stop US-Israeli strikes
