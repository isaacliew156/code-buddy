# Federal Reserve Policy 2026

The Federal Reserve's monetary policy in 2026 was characterized by balancing conflicting pressures of persistent inflation, oil shock impacts, and war-related economic uncertainty.

## Current Policy Stance (April 2026)
- **Federal Funds Rate**: 3.50%-3.75% (held steady for two consecutive meetings)
- **Previous Rate Moves**: Three rate cuts in late 2025 brought rates down from 4.75%-5.00%

## March 2026 FOMC Decision
- **Vote**: 11-1 to hold rates
- **Dissent**: Stephen I. Miran preferred 25 basis point cut
- **Powell Statement**: Described economy as facing "an energy shock of some size and duration"

## Economic Projections (March 2026)
- **GDP Growth**: 2.4% for 2026 (revised up slightly)
- **Inflation (PCE)**: 2.7% for 2026 (revised up for both headline and core)
- **Unemployment**: 4.4% expected by year-end
- **Dot Plot**: Median still points to one rate cut in 2026 and another in 2027

## Key Policy Tensions

### Inflation Pressures
- Inflation remains above 2% target
- Oil prices threaten to push inflation higher
- Core and headline PCE both revised upward

### Economic Headwinds
- Labor market softening with low job gains
- Iran war adds significant economic uncertainty
- Energy shock affecting consumer spending and production

### Market Expectations
- Markets price meaningful odds of no cuts at all in 2026
- Scenarios with multiple cuts also considered
- Deep uncertainty about forward path

## Additional Factors

### Leadership Transition
- Jerome Powell's term as Fed Chair expires May 15, 2026
- Adds another layer of policy uncertainty

### Bond Market Dynamics
- 10-year Treasury yield: ~4.31-4.34% (briefly touched 4.48% in late March)
- 30-year yield: Briefly hit 5% before pulling back
