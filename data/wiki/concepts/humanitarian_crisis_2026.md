# Humanitarian Crisis (2026 Iran War)

## Overview
The 2026 Iran War has created a multifaceted humanitarian crisis affecting Iran, Lebanon, and regional populations through direct combat, infrastructure destruction, and economic collapse.

## Iranian Civilian Impact

### Casualties
- **2,076 killed** in Iran since war began (as of April 7, 2026)
- **26,500 wounded** in Iran
- **600+ schools and education centers** hit by strikes
- **Hospitals**: Multiple medical facilities damaged or destroyed

### Infrastructure Destruction
- **Pasteur Institute**: Century-old medical research center destroyed
- **Mahshahr petrochemical complex**: 70% of domestic gasoline production capacity crippled
- **Bridges and roads**: Transportation infrastructure targeted
- **Red Crescent aid warehouse**: Humanitarian supplies destroyed

### Information Blackout
- **January 2026**: Near-total internet blackout imposed
- **Continuing through war**: Internet remains almost entirely shut down
- **Impact**: Prevents documentation of atrocities, hinders aid coordination

## Lebanese Parallel Conflict

### Hezbollah Escalation
- **Conflict scale**: Parallel war with Israel
- **Casualties**: Over 1,400 killed in Lebanon
- **Displacement**: More than 1 million displaced (roughly one-sixth of population)

### Government Response
- **March 2026**: Lebanese government moves to ban Hezbollah's military wing
- **Demands**: Calls for Hezbollah to surrender weapons
- **Sovereignty challenge**: Government attempts to assert control over militia

### Civilian Impact
- **Infrastructure damage**: Residential areas and civilian facilities hit
- **Refugee crisis**: Mass internal displacement
- **Economic collapse**: War compounds existing Lebanese economic crisis

## Regional Civilian Casualties

### Gulf States
- **Foreign workers**: Casualties among expatriate populations in UAE
- **Civilian infrastructure**: Attacks on non-military targets
- **Psychological trauma**: Population living under missile threat

### Israel
- **6,833 casualties** evacuated to Israeli hospitals
- **Civilian deaths**: Two killed in Ramat Gan missile attack (March 17, 2026)
- **Economic disruption**: Daily life interrupted by air raid sirens

## Political Prisoner Executions

### Regime Actions
- **January 2026 onward**: Accelerating pace of executions
- **Examples**: 19-year-old Mohammad-Amin Biglari executed (April 5, 2026)
- **Purposes**: Internal security, eliminating opposition, maintaining control

### International Response
- **Condemnation**: International human rights organizations
- **Diplomatic pressure**: Used as justification for continued sanctions
- **Moral dimension**: Undermines regime's humanitarian claims

## Energy Crisis Impacts

### Food and Medicine Shortages
- **Supply chain disruption**: Strait of Hormuz closure affects all imports
- **Medical supplies**: Critical shortages in hospitals
- **Fuel shortages**: Transportation and heating affected

### Global Spillover
- **Oil prices**: Above $100/barrel affects global poor
- **Inflation**: Food price increases worldwide
- **Developing nations**: Most vulnerable to energy price shocks

## International Aid Challenges

### Access Restrictions
- **Internet blackout**: Hinders needs assessment
- **Security situation**: Active combat prevents aid delivery
- **Political barriers**: Sanctions complicate humanitarian exceptions

### UN Response
- **Security Council**: Focused on military rather than humanitarian issues
- **Funding gaps**: Multiple simultaneous crises strain resources
- **Coordination challenges**: Multiple actors with differing agendas

## Long-Term Consequences

### Health System Collapse
- **Hospital capacity**: Overwhelmed by casualties
- **Medical staff**: Working under extreme conditions
- **Disease risk**: Sanitation and clean water compromised

### Education Disruption
- **School closures**: 600+ facilities damaged
- **Generational impact**: Lost educational years
- **Psychological trauma**: Children experiencing war

### Economic Devastation
- **GDP contraction**: World Bank projected negative growth for 2025-2026
- **Unemployment**: Structural damage to economy
- **Reconstruction costs**: Billions needed for infrastructure
