# Strait of Hormuz Crisis

## Overview
The Strait of Hormuz crisis began when Iran closed the strategic waterway during the 2026 Iran War, halting most shipping and causing global economic disruption.

## Key Facts
- **Location**: Connects Persian Gulf with Gulf of Oman and Arabian Sea
- **Strategic importance**: Approximately 20% of world's oil and LNG passes through waterway
- **Closure date**: Early March 2026 (following Operation Epic Fury)
- **Duration**: 38+ days as of April 7, 2026

## Economic Impact
- **Oil prices**: Surged above $100 per barrel (60% increase in some markets)
- **LNG disruption**: Iran's attacks destroyed 17% of Qatar's LNG capacity for up to 5 years
- **Shipping**: Companies avoiding strait entirely
- **Global supply chains**: Disruptions expected to persist long after reopening

## US Response
- **April 6, 2026**: Trump set Tuesday (April 8) deadline for Iran to reopen strait
- **Threat**: Trump warned of strikes on Iranian infrastructure if deadline not met
- **Military planning**: UK hosting talks with ~40 countries on reopening strait
- **Regional support**: UAE expressed willingness to join international force

## Iranian Position
- **Demands**: Permanent end to hostilities before reopening
- **Proposal**: Protocol for safe passage through strait as part of ceasefire terms
- **Leverage**: Using strait closure as bargaining chip in negotiations

## International Responses
- **Gulf Cooperation Council**: Called for UN Security Council resolution authorizing force
- **UN Security Council**: March 11 resolution reaffirmed freedom of navigation
- **China**: Continued purchasing Iranian oil but declined military involvement
- **Russia**: Benefiting from elevated oil prices

## Regional Effects
- **UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain**: Suffered direct Iranian attacks despite not being belligerents
- **Qatar**: Lost significant LNG capacity
- **Energy security**: Global dependence on Persian Gulf oil exposed

## Timeline
- **March 2026**: Iran closes strait after Operation Epic Fury begins
- **March 11, 2026**: UNSC resolution reaffirms freedom of navigation
- **March 16, 2026**: China and NATO reject Trump's call for military support
- **April 1, 2026**: UK begins talks with ~40 countries on reopening
- **April 8, 2026**: Trump's deadline for Iran to reopen
