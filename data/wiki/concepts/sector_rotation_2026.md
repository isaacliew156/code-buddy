# Sector Rotation 2026

The Iran War triggered one of the most dramatic sector rotations in recent market history, with investors fleeing growth/tech stocks and rotating into defensive and energy-related sectors.

## Top Performing Sectors

### Energy Sector
- **Performance**: +18% in March 2026 alone
- **Drivers**: Direct result of oil price shock from Strait of Hormuz closure
- **Context**: Top-performing sector for the period

### Defense/Industrials
- **Key Stocks**:
  - RTX: +22% in March 2026
  - Lockheed Martin: +19% in March 2026
  - Northrop Grumman: +17% in March 2026
- **Drivers**: Increased military spending and geopolitical tensions

### Defensive Sectors
- **Utilities**: Outperformed as defensive haven
- **Real Estate**: Also outperformed during market volatility

## Worst Performing Sectors

### Consumer Discretionary
- **Performance**: -12% in March 2026 (worst performer)
- **Drivers**:
  - Soaring fuel costs from oil price shock
  - Recession fears affecting consumer spending
  - Higher input costs for manufacturers

### Technology
- **Underperformance**: QQQ (Invesco Nasdaq-100 ETF) down ~2.8% YTD
- **Drivers**:
  - "AI exhaustion" concerns about ROI on massive AI capital expenditures
  - Elevated tech valuations becoming unattractive in rising rate environment
  - Market rotation out of growth stocks

## Divergence Patterns
The divergence between value/energy and growth/tech has been one of the defining features of 2026 financial markets:
- **Value vs. Growth**: Classic risk-off rotation
- **Cyclical vs. Defensive**: Flight to safety and essential services
- **Energy vs. Consumer**: Direct impact of oil shock on related sectors

## ETF Performance Themes
- **Winners**: Energy ETFs, defense-sector ETFs
- **Losers**: Tech-heavy and growth-oriented funds
- **Broader Market**: VOO (Vanguard S&P 500 ETF) down ~0.6% YTD due to diversification
