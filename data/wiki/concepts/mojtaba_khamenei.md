# Mojtaba Khamenei

## Overview
Mojtaba Khamenei is the son of the late Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei, elected as Iran's new Supreme Leader on March 8, 2026, following his father's death in the initial Operation Epic Fury strikes.

## Rise to Power
- **March 1, 2026**: Interim Leadership Council established under Iran's constitution to govern until new Supreme Leader elected
- **March 8, 2026**: Mojtaba Khamenei elected as new Supreme Leader
- **March 11, 2026**: IRGC and top officials pledge allegiance to him

## Leadership Context
Mojtaba assumes leadership during:
- Ongoing war with US-Israel coalition (Operation Epic Fury)
- Internal leadership paralysis and power struggles
- Near-total internet blackout in Iran
- Accelerated executions of political prisoners
- Economic collapse with 60% annual inflation

## Governance Challenges
- Regime decision-making described as "fractured" and "paralyzed"
- Communications breakdown within leadership
- Internal power struggles among remaining officials
- Maintaining control over IRGC and security forces

## Foreign Relations
Mojtaba's government:
- Rejected Pakistan's 45-day ceasefire proposal
- Presented 10-point plan demanding permanent end to hostilities
- Maintained closure of Strait of Hormuz
- Continued missile and drone attacks on regional targets

## Analysis
Leadership characterized by:
- Paranoia and suspicion among remaining officials
- Severely disrupted decision-making processes
- Uncertain authority over military and security apparatus
- Inheriting governance during humanitarian crisis
