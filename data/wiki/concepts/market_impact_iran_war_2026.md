# Market Impact of Iran War 2026

The Iran War that began on February 28, 2026 ("Operation Epic Fury") has been the defining market event of 2026, driving a sharp correction across global financial markets and triggering dramatic sector rotations.

## Key Market Effects

### Stock Market Correction
- **S&P 500**: Fell ~5-6% from January 27, 2026 all-time high of 7,002.38 to ~6,612 by April 7, 2026
- **Nasdaq Composite**: Entered correction territory (>10% decline), falling as much as 13.4% from October 2025 peak of 24,019.99 to ~21,996
- **Dow Jones Industrial Average**: Also entered correction territory, dropping to ~46,670
- **VIX (Volatility Index)**: Elevated to ~24, up from February average of ~16

### "Stealth Bear Market"
The average S&P 500 stock was down ~21% from its 52-week high, creating a "stealth bear market" beneath surface-level index declines.

### Relief Rally
A four-day winning streak through April 7 offered some relief, driven by reports of potential 45-day ceasefire proposals between US, Iran, and regional mediators.

## ETF Performance
- **SPY**: Negative year-to-date, reflecting broader S&P 500 decline
- **QQQ**: Underperformed with ~2.8% YTD decline due to AI valuation concerns
- **VOO**: Down ~0.6% YTD, slightly better than QQQ due to diversification

## Market Response Timeline
- **February 28, 2026**: Operation Epic Fury begins - initial market impact
- **March 4, 2026**: Iran closes Strait of Hormuz - oil shock begins
- **Late March**: Broad market correction deepens, indices enter correction territory
- **March 27**: Oil surges above $110/barrel
- **April 2**: Trump vows continued operations for 2-3 more weeks; oil jumps 12% to $112.10
- **Early April**: Markets rally on ceasefire speculation
