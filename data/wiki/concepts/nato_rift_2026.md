# NATO Rift 2026

## Overview
The 2026 NATO rift is a severe strain in transatlantic relations resulting from European allies' refusal to support US military operations against Iran.

## Core Issues
- European allies broadly refused to join military operations against Iran
- US launched Operation Epic Fury without consultation with NATO allies
- No international legal framework for the war
- Divergent views on justifiability of military action

## European Positions by Country

### Spain
- Called the war "unjustifiable"
- Denied military support

### France
- Denied airspace for weapons shipments to Israel
- Supported diplomatic resolution over military action

### Italy
- Denied base access for US bombers
- Refused operational support

### Poland
- Refused to relocate Patriot air defense systems to Middle East
- Limited cooperation to defensive missions only

### United Kingdom
- Allowed limited use of bases for defensive missions only
- Hosting diplomatic talks about Strait of Hormuz
- Not participating in offensive operations

## US Response
- **Trump**: Called NATO a "paper tiger"
- **Trump**: Threatened US withdrawal from alliance
- **Secretary of State Rubio**: Said US would "reexamine the value of NATO"
- **Trump**: Also rebuked Japan, South Korea, and Australia for not joining war

## NATO Official Actions
- **Missile defense**: Intercepted Iranian missile that entered Turkish airspace (landed in Hatay Province)
- **Defense commitment**: Secretary General Mark Rutte affirmed commitment to defending Turkey
- **Alliance coordination**: No unified position on Iran operations

## Analysis
The rift has exposed:
- **Strategic divergence**: Different threat perceptions and policy approaches
- **Legitimacy gap**: US unilateral action undermines alliance consensus
- **Burden sharing**: Questions about US commitment to European security
- **Future planning**: European need for independent defense capability

## Strategic Implications
- **European security**: Need for independent defense architecture without US
- **Timeline**: Building European capability would take years
- **Vulnerability**: Europe exposed during transition period
- **Russian threat**: Ongoing threat from Russia during NATO weakness

## Historical Context
Described as:
- Deepest rift in NATO's 77-year history
- Unprecedented refusal of European allies to support US military action
- Potential beginning of NATO realignment
