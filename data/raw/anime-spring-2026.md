# Anime Spring 2026 Season — Comprehensive Report

*Last updated: April 7, 2026*

## 1. Top Anime of Spring 2026

Spring 2026 is shaping up to be one of the most stacked seasons in recent memory, with a mix of massive returning franchises and exciting new adaptations. Here are the standout titles dominating the season.

### Re:Zero — Starting Life in Another World Season 4
**Studio:** White Fox | **Episodes:** 19 (split-cour) | **Premiere:** April 8, 2026 (Crunchyroll)

The undisputed king of the season by fan vote. Both the Dengeki Online and Anime Corner surveys crowned Re:Zero Season 4 as the most anticipated anime of Spring 2026, with over 10% of votes and 122,750 MAL members tracking it. This season adapts the Desert Tower arc, following Subaru's journey to recover his stolen name and memories at the Pleiades Watchtower. The 19 episodes are split into two arcs: The Loss Arc (11 episodes, April–June) and The Recapture Arc (8 episodes, starting August 12). The series currently holds an 8.24/10 user score on MAL, making it the second-highest rated isekai anime on the platform.

### Dorohedoro Season 2
**Studio:** MAPPA | **Premiere:** April 1, 2026 (3-episode premiere)

One of the most exciting surprise returns of the season. After a six-year wait since Season 1, MAPPA delivered a big three-episode premiere that immediately captured fan attention. The series continues the story of Caiman, the amnesiac with a lizard head searching for the sorcerer who cursed him. The original season was widely praised for its blend of 3D and 2D animation that faithfully captured the manga's detailed art style.

### Witch Hat Atelier
**Studio:** BUG FILMS | **Director:** Ayumu Watanabe | **Premiere:** April 2026

Based on Kamome Shirahama's Eisner Award-winning manga, this is arguably the most anticipated new adaptation of the season. The story follows Coco, a girl who discovers that magic is drawn with special ink rather than inherited, and becomes an apprentice witch to undo a spell that petrified her mother. Early theatrical screenings of the first two episodes drew enthusiastic praise. The pressure on BUG FILMS to match the manga's famously gorgeous artwork is immense, but early signs are very promising.

### That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime Season 4
**Studio:** 8bit | **Premiere:** April 2026

Topped the Animate Times poll as Japan's most-anticipated Spring 2026 anime. Rimuru's empire faces new opposition from the Demon Lord council as the story continues to expand its political and military scope. With over 111,270 MAL members tracking it, TenSura remains one of the isekai genre's heaviest hitters.

### Classroom of the Elite Season 4
**Studio:** Lerche | **Premiere:** April 2026

The psychological thriller continues its run as one of the season's most tracked series, with over 109,091 MAL members. Lerche has become synonymous with the franchise, and each season has improved on the last in terms of visual quality and tension.

### Steel Ball Run: JoJo's Bizarre Adventure (Continuing)
**Studio:** David Production | **Platform:** Netflix | **Premiere:** March 19, 2026

While technically a Winter premiere, Steel Ball Run continues into Spring and is one of the biggest anime events of the year. This sixth animated season of JoJo's adapts the beloved seventh part of Hirohiko Araki's manga. David Production has been building toward this adaptation for 14 years, and it's considered the crown jewel of the entire franchise.

## 4. Biggest Surprises & Breakout Hits

### Dorohedoro Season 2
Nobody expected MAPPA to deliver this after six years of silence. The three-episode premiere on April 1 immediately made it one of the most talked-about shows of the season.

### Witch Hat Atelier
The early theatrical screenings generated major buzz, with critics describing it as a visual spectacle. The combination of a beloved source material and passionate staff has positioned it as a potential all-time great adaptation.

### Daemons of the Shadow Realm
Hiromu Arakawa's name alone generates hype, but the first episode apparently contains a major twist around the halfway mark that has been kept under wraps. Early reactions describe it as a genuinely surprising left-turn that elevates the show beyond its initial setup.

### Dandadan's Award Season Dominance
While technically a Winter carryover, Dandadan Season 2 received 20 nominations at the 2026 Crunchyroll Anime Awards — more than any other series — including a frontrunner position for Anime of the Year. This is especially notable given that Solo Leveling Season 2, despite breaking platform records, was snubbed entirely for AOTY.

### Fire Force's Series Finale
The final episode of Fire Force Season 4 tied the entire story to Atsushi Ohkubo's other iconic manga in a conclusion that was exactly as over-the-top as fans expected. It was one of the most discussed finales of Winter 2026.

### Liar Game
A decades-old manga finally getting animated, and it's earning attention for its focus on pure psychological warfare and game theory — a refreshing departure from the physical combat that dominates most shonen.

## 5. Notable Industry News

### Toei Animation's AI Controversy
Toei announced plans to integrate AI into their production pipeline, sparking immediate backlash from fans who feared animators would be replaced. Toei later clarified that AI would not take over animators' roles, but the announcement tapped into broader industry anxiety about AI's growing presence in anime production.

### Crunchyroll Subscription Price Increase
Crunchyroll raised subscription prices during the middle of Jujutsu Kaisen Season 3's run, drawing significant criticism. Combined with canceled Right Stuf back orders for older series, many fans questioned whether the platform respects its paying audience.

### One Piece Goes Seasonal
One Piece is officially transitioning from weekly episodes to a seasonal format in 2026, with a maximum of 26 episodes split into two cours. This is a direct response to rising quality expectations and production sustainability concerns.

### Studio Bankruptcies Continue
According to Teikoku Databank, eight animation production groups in Japan closed operations in 2025, with at least two declaring bankruptcy. The trend underscores the precarious financial state of many studios despite anime's global popularity boom.

### The Rise of Nostalgia Remakes
An industry trend report by Bushiroad's Anime Data Insights Lab predicts that remakes of '90s and early 2000s anime will "accelerate even further" in 2026. Reboots of Magic Knight Rayearth and High School! Kimengumi are already confirmed. The trend is driven by the purchasing power of millennial and Gen X fans in their 30s and 40s.

### Solo Leveling's Awards Snub
Despite Solo Leveling Season 2 breaking numerous records on Crunchyroll, it was not nominated for Anime of the Year at the 2026 Crunchyroll Anime Awards, making it the biggest snub of the year. The manhwa's Volume 24 hit #1 in Japan regardless.

### Streaming Wars Intensify
Crunchyroll secured exclusive rights to the majority of Spring 2026's major titles, creating a near-monopoly on the season. Netflix and HIDIVE are reportedly investing heavily in Summer 2026 exclusives to counter this dominance. The days of one platform having everything are definitively over.

### Industry-Wide Shift Toward Proven IPs
The Bushiroad report also warns that studios are increasingly greenlit only productions with "clear track records," crowding out original and innovative storytelling. Combined with genre saturation in isekai and shonen, some analysts worry about creative stagnation despite the market's financial growth.
